Societies are non-profit organisations that are established for promoting cultural, educational, social, or charitable activities. Society registration is a legal process that provides recognition to the organisation and makes it easier to operate and raise funds. The said societies are governed by the Society Registration Act, 1860 and said act is amended several times. The societies are formed to maintain residential or commercial complexes or to create the apartment association.

Society Registration Process

To register a society in India, there are certain steps that need to be followed. The first step is to choose a unique name for the society, which should not be identical or similar to any other organisation or society registered in the country. The next step is to prepare a Memorandum of Association (MoA) that outlines the objectives, rules, and regulations of the society. The MoA details such as the name and address of the society, the aims and objectives, the governing body, the members, and their roles and responsibilities. Once the MoA and bylaws are ready, the application for society registration should be submitted to the Registrar of Societies in the state where the society is to be registered. The application should include the MoA, bylaws, list of office bearers, and a statement of the assets and liabilities of the society.
