What is Private Limited Company ?

Requirement to incorporate Private Limited Company

To register a private limited company, several steps need to be followed to ensure a seamless and hassle-free process. The first step is to obtain a DIN (Director Identification Number) and a DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) for the proposed directors of the company. These are essential requirements for the registration process and can be obtained by submitting the necessary documents to the relevant authorities. The next step is to choose a unique company name and get it approved by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA). This is important as it ensures that the company has a distinct identity and can differentiate itself from competitors. The name approval process can be completed online, and our team can assist you in this process. Once the name is approved, the next step is to file the incorporation documents, including the Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association, with the MCA. Our team of experts can assist you in drafting these documents and ensure that they comply with all the necessary legal requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

A private limited company is a type of company in which the liability of the shareholders is limited to the amount of shares they hold in the company.

To register a private limited company, you need to have a minimum of two directors and two shareholders, a unique company name, and registered office address.

The process of registering a private limited company involves obtaining a DIN and DSC for the directors, choosing a unique company name, filing the incorporation documents with the MCA, obtaining the certificate of incorporation, and obtaining PAN, TAN, and GST registration.

The time taken to register a private limited company varies depending on the documents submitted and the processing time of the MCA. Typically, it takes between 10-15 working days to register a private limited company.

Some of the benefits of registering a private limited company include limited liability, separate legal entity, easy transferability of shares, credibility, and tax benefits.
